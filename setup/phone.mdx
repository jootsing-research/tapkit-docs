## Step 1: Plug in your iPhone

Connect your iPhone to your Mac via USB. The Mac app detects the phone automatically.

## Step 2: Trust and authorize

You may see one or both of these prompts:

- **On iPhone:** "Trust This Computer?" — tap **Trust** and enter your passcode
- **On Mac:** "Allow this phone to connect?" — click **Allow**

<Note>
  These prompts don't always appear (especially if you've connected before). If the Mac app doesn't recognize your phone after approving, unplug and replug the USB cable.
</Note>

## Step 3: Headphone popup

A popup appears on your iPhone asking if the connected device is headphones or another device. Tap **Other Device**.

{/* TODO: Screenshot of the headphone popup */}

<Info>
  **Why this happens:** TapKit uses screen sharing to see the phone, which routes audio through the Mac. iOS interprets this as an audio device connection. This is normal Apple system behavior.
</Info>

### Things that might look weird

Don't worry if you notice these — they're cosmetic:

- **Clock shows 9:41** — your phone enters Apple's presentation mode because of the screencast. This is normal.
- **Headphone icon in status bar** — same audio routing reason. Goes away when you disconnect.

## Step 4: Run the setup Shortcut

The Mac app shows a QR code on screen. Scan it with your iPhone camera. This opens the TapKit setup Shortcut.

Tap **Run** to execute the Shortcut. It automatically configures a series of iOS settings:

- Switch Control settings and scanning mode
- Platform switching for remote control
- Accessibility preferences
- Menu item ordering for gesture automation

{/* TODO: Screenshot of QR code in Mac app and Shortcut running on iPhone */}

<Note>
  The setup Shortcut only changes Switch Control and accessibility settings. You can inspect exactly what it does by tapping the three-dot menu on the Shortcut in the Shortcuts app.
</Note>

## Step 5: Enable Switch Control

At the end of the setup Shortcut, you'll be directed to manually turn on Switch Control:

1. Go to **Settings > Accessibility > Switch Control**
2. Toggle **Switch Control** to **On** at the top of the screen
3. The gliding cursor should appear on screen

<Warning>
  Switch Control must be on for TapKit to work. If you turn it off, TapKit won't be able to control the phone.
</Warning>

## Step 6: Add the TapKit Shortcut

Scan the second QR code shown in the Mac app. This opens the TapKit website in a logged-in state.

1. Tap **Add Shortcut** to install the TapKit runtime Shortcut
2. When prompted, paste your **Shortcut token** (displayed on the setup page)
3. Run the Shortcut once and approve any permission popups

  **About the Shortcut token:** This is a restricted API key with limited read-only access. It cannot modify your account settings or access sensitive data. See [Security & Privacy](/security) for details.

<Warning>
  The TapKit Shortcut must be the **first shortcut** in your Shortcuts app. If you have other shortcuts, open the Shortcuts app, tap Edit, and drag TapKit to the first position.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Phone not detected after plugging in">
    Unplug the USB cable, wait a few seconds, and plug it back in. Make sure to approve any "Trust This Computer?" or "Allow" prompts that appear on either device.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Phone not on Wi-Fi">
    Your phone can look like it's on Wi-Fi but not actually be connected. Go to **Settings > Wi-Fi** on your iPhone and explicitly tap your network to reconnect. This is the most common troubleshooting issue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="QR code won't scan">
    Make sure your iPhone camera is in photo mode (not video or portrait). Hold the phone steady about 6-8 inches from the screen. If the QR code still won't scan, try increasing your Mac's screen brightness.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Setup Shortcut fails midway">
    Run the Shortcut again — it's safe to re-run. If it continues to fail, make sure you're running iOS 16 or later and that you have the Shortcuts app installed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't add untrusted Shortcuts">
    Go to **Settings > Shortcuts** on your iPhone and enable **Allow Running Scripts** and **Private Sharing**. This allows installing Shortcuts from sources other than the Shortcuts Gallery.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Headphone popup keeps reappearing">
    This can happen if the screencast connection drops and reconnects. Tap **Other Device** each time. If it happens frequently, make sure your USB cable is securely connected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

If you're experiencing issues not covered here, [contact our support team](mailto:support@tapkit.ai).
